Mikio Tada

I'm a first-year Biomedical Science PhD student at Icahn School of Medicine at Mount Sinai.

I'm interested in using deep learning to address clinical and biological questions broadly. My research involves developing computational models to analyze healthcare and biomedical data, with the aim of contributing to advancements in personalized medicine and improving patient care.

Previously, I was a Research specialist at UCSF, where I worked under the mentorship of Michael Keiser and Maria Wei. I earned my MSc in Data Science from the University of San Francisco and my BSc in Mathematics from Juniata College.

Background

As an international student, I came to the US with limited English skills compared to my fluency in Japanese, which led to many challenges during my undergraduate studies. I spent my freshman year learning English as a second language. Initially inspired to become a diplomat, I studied abroad in The Gambia between my freshman and sophomore years. However, this experience shifted my focus towards technology, and I eventually transitioned into Computational Biomedicine. I’m happy to connect with anyone considering studying abroad in the US, applying for a biomedical-related PhD with a computational background but limited experience in biomedicine, or simply interested in discussing research. Echoing Rachel Thomas’ words, You are best positioned to help people one step behind you.
